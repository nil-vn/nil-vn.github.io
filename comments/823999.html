During the closing process, the project manager must verify that all deliverables meet the agreed-upon requirements and customer expectations. PMBOK recommends validating project deliverables against documented acceptance criteria to determine whether the concern is valid or if the deliverable has already been formally accepted. This approach ensures objective decision-making rather than immediately negotiating or escalating the issue. PMBOK Key Processes Referenced: Validate Scope (Ensuring Deliverables Meet Requirements) Control Quality (Verifying Deliverables Before Closing) Close Project or Phase (Ensuring Formal Acceptance Before Closure)

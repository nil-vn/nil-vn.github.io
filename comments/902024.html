When a key stakeholder like the business lead is unavailable, the best course of action is to engage a delegate who can represent them in meetings, provide insights, and ensure continuity in decision-making. By doing this, the project manager: Maintains momentum in execution Ensures that business input is available early in the process Respects the business lead’s schedule while still involving the business function appropriately Delegation is a common and effective practice in project management when key roles are temporarily unavailable.

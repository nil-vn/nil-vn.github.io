Uncontrolled scope changes (scope creep) can lead to project failure. According to PMBOK, all change requests must follow the change control process, and unauthorized changes must be addressed. The Communications Management Plan defines who communicates what, to whom, and through which channels. By directly addressing the director, the project manager can: Reinforce proper change control procedures Clarify roles and authority in decision-making Prevent confusion and misalignment within the project team PMBOK Key Processes Referenced: Manage Communications (Executing Process Group) Perform Integrated Change Control (Change Management Process) Stakeholder Engagement & Governance Policies

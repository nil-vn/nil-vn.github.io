Selected Answer: C
C. Acknowledge the project closure criteria has been met and release the remaining budget and resources. Explanation: Once the project has met the agreed-upon requirements and the closure criteria have been fulfilled, the project should formally close, regardless of leftover time or budget. Continuing to add functionality (often referred to as "scope creep") without a clear and justified business need can introduce unnecessary risks, compromise the agreed scope, and conflict with project management best practices.

Selected Answer: C
The project manager should **acknowledge the project closure criteria has been met and release the remaining budget and resources** (Option C). Once the agreed-upon product requirements are met and the project is closing, it's important to follow the project closure process. This includes releasing the remaining budget and resources so they can be allocated to other projects. Adding additional functionality or conducting additional quality checks may not align with the original project scope and could lead to scope creep. It's important to stick to the agreed-upon project plan and scope, and any changes should go through the proper change control process.

To ensure proper communication with the stakeholder who has been promoted to lead another functional area, the project manager should have the project team update the stakeholder register. The stakeholder register is a key document that lists all stakeholders involved in the project and their roles, responsibilities, and communication requirements. The register should be updated whenever there are changes in stakeholder roles or responsibilities. In this situation, the stakeholder who has been promoted to lead another functional area is still a stakeholder in the project. Therefore, the stakeholder register should be updated to reflect their new role and any changes in their communication requirements. Updating the project management plan or the stakeholder engagement plan may also be necessary, but the stakeholder register is the most appropriate document to update in this situation. The communications management plan outlines how project communications will be managed, but the stakeholder register is the document that lists all stakeholders and their communication requirements.

The key part of the question is "to ensure proper communications to stakeholders". Updating the Stakeholder register is an important initial step because it keeps stakeholder information current. Recording the new role and any updated contact details ensures the project team has accurate data. However, this alone doesn’t guarantee that communication remains effective—it’s a record, not a strategy. The Communications management plan, on the other hand, builds on stakeholder information to define how communication occurs. If the promoted stakeholder’s new position alters their communication needs—such as frequency, format, or content—adjusting this plan ensures those needs are met. Since the question focuses on "proper communications" and implies action to maintain effectiveness across all stakeholders, the Communications management plan is the most directly relevant document. It uses updated stakeholder data (from the register) to adapt communication strategies as needed.

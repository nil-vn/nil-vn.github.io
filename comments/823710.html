Answer: A The project manager should first review the construction project to determine which agile practices can be applied in this particular context. Not all agile practices will be applicable to a construction project, and it's important to choose those that are appropriate. Once the applicable practices have been identified, the project manager can apply them accordingly to the project. Hiring a consultant or allocating additional budget for agile resources may not be necessary if the project manager has the necessary knowledge and expertise to apply agile practices appropriately. Similarly, assigning a scrum master may not be necessary if the project manager is already familiar with agile practices and can implement them without additional support.

Selected Answer: D
The situation describes an incremental approach ("latest increment approved on the current iteration"). In such environments, regular reviews are vital. When a new sponsor enters, especially one not previously engaged, the project manager's immediate priority should be to bring them fully up to speed on the project's progress, vision, and the value delivered. A product iteration review (or a series of them, if necessary) would be the most effective way to achieve this, allowing the new sponsor to understand what has been built and approve the current increment, thereby preventing the wholesale rejection of artifacts and rebuilding confidence. While a stakeholder analysis (A) is a good foundation, it's the action of engaging the sponsor through specific mechanisms like a detailed review that truly prevents the described issues. The final answer is {D}

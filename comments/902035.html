In an Agile environment, the sprint planning meeting is where the team and product owner collaborate to determine which backlog items will be worked on in the upcoming sprint. If the product owner indicates a need to change the scope of the first release, they should create new backlog items based on the updated requirements and bring them to the next sprint planning meeting for discussion and prioritization. This allows the team to review, understand, and plan how to incorporate the new items into the upcoming sprint's work.

As the project is already in the execution phase and likely working with an established backlog, the project lead should prioritize reviewing the existing backlog items included in the release. This step is crucial for understanding the current status of the project and identifying which items are affected by the proposed scope changes. Once the priorities of the existing backlog items are reviewed and aligned with the proposed changes, the project lead and the product owner can then proceed to discuss how to incorporate the new scope or adjust the backlog items in subsequent planning or retrospective meetings.

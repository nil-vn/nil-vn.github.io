Selected Answer: D
If an identified risk materializes (such as the regulatory labels being missed), the risk response plan within the risk management plan should already outline the actions to be taken. This includes the immediate steps to address the issue, who is responsible, and any budget or schedule implications. Therefore, while reviewing the quality management plan is necessary to address the immediate quality lapse, reviewing the risk management plan is equally important because it should contain the predetermined risk responses for such scenarios. If a response strategy is already documented for this specific risk in the risk management plan, the project manager should follow that prescribed response. This course of action aligns with the PMBOK 7th Edition’s emphasis on a dynamic and adaptive project management approach, where planning and execution are responsive to changes and new information. The project manager should utilize the risk management plan to execute the appropriate response actions swiftly and effectively.

Since this is a multiyear initiative involving cross-functional stakeholders, the first priority in the planning phase is to clearly define the project's purpose, objectives, and scope before diving into specific plans. The business case justifies the project’s existence, showing how it aligns with business goals and expected benefits. The scope document defines what is included (and excluded) in the project, ensuring all stakeholders have a shared understanding of deliverables. Without a solid business case and scope, other planning efforts (like backlog creation, communication, or risk management) may be misaligned. Developing a communications management plan is important but secondary—you need a defined scope first to determine what needs to be communicated.

In the planning phase, the first step is to define why the project exists (business case) and what the project will deliver (scope document). A business case outlines the project's value, objectives, and justification, ensuring stakeholder alignment. A scope document (Project Scope Statement) helps define deliverables, boundaries, and key requirements before developing detailed plans. PMBOK recommends starting with a clear business and scope definition before creating detailed plans (such as risk or communication plans). PMBOK Key Processes Referenced: Develop Project Charter & Business Case (Initiating Process Group) Define Scope (Planning Process Group) Ensure Stakeholder Alignment Before Creating Detailed Plans

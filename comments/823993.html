Selected Answer: A
The key to engaging a high-level stakeholder, such as a member of the board of directors, is to respect their time and provide them with concise, relevant information. Option A suggests keeping the stakeholder informed, which means regularly updating them with the most crucial and relevant information about the project. By doing this, the project manager ensures that the stakeholder remains in the loop without being burdened by unnecessary details. Additionally, consulting with them based on their needs allows the stakeholder to provide input and direction when they feel it's necessary, thus ensuring that their perspective and priorities are taken into account. Given the many responsibilities and priorities of a board member, this approach strikes a balance between keeping them engaged and not overwhelming them with information.

Selected Answer: A
A. Keep the stakeholder informed and consult with them based on their needs is the best approach. This will ensure that the stakeholder is kept up to date on the project's progress and can provide input or guidance when necessary. It is important to consult with the stakeholder to understand their needs and priorities and to align the project with those as much as possible. Additionally, involving the stakeholder in governance and decision making (B) and increasing their level of interest (C) can also be effective in keeping them engaged in the project. Making the stakeholder aware of the project progress (D) is also important, but it is not sufficient by itself to ensure engagement.

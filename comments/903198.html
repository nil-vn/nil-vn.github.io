Before engaging with potential suppliers or negotiating prices, the project manager should first identify the policies, procedures, guidelines, and legal parameters that need to be considered in the contract. This involves understanding the regulatory and legal requirements, as well as any specific policies or guidelines that govern the procurement process. By doing so, the project manager can ensure that the future contract not only secures a good price but also aligns with legal and organizational standards, promoting a fair and compliant procurement process. This foundational step sets the groundwork for a successful and value-driven contract negotiation process.

Answer B is the most appropriate in this situation. The project manager should inform the owner that a rough order of magnitude will be provided. This approach will give the owner an initial idea of the costs involved, but it will not be a detailed estimate. The project manager can then work with the project team and subject matter experts to refine the estimate and provide a more accurate cost estimate. Calling a meeting, contacting a subject matter expert, or telling the owner that the task requires more time and expertise will not help the project manager to complete the task within the given timeframe.

In agile projects, it's important to be flexible and adapt to the specific circumstances of the project. If a vendor is unable to deliver components in an incremental, collaborative way, the project manager can allow the vendor to use an alternative approach, such as a traditional delivery method. The key is to find a solution that allows the project to proceed while accommodating the constraints or limitations of external contributors. Integrating the vendor's components into the overall project can still follow agile principles, even if the vendor's delivery method differs.

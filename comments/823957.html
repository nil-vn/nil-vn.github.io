By conducting a stakeholder analysis, the project manager can identify the key stakeholders, understand their needs, expectations, and influence on the project. This helps in prioritizing the changes and managing stakeholder expectations more effectively. It ensures that the project addresses the most critical and impactful changes, thus reducing unnecessary modifications and aligning the project scope with stakeholder priorities. Adopting an incremental approach, such as Agile or iterative development, allows the project to deliver smaller portions of the project incrementally. This method helps in managing changes more effectively, as it allows for regular reassessment and adaptation. By breaking the project into manageable increments, the team can implement changes and gather feedback continuously, thus minimizing the risk of large-scale reworks and helping to keep the project on track with time and budget constraints.

The project manager should opt for **A. Conduct a stakeholder analysis** and **D. Adopt an incremental approach**. **A. Conduct a stakeholder analysis**: This can help the project manager understand the needs and expectations of different stakeholders, which can in turn help in managing the changes requested by them. By understanding who is requesting changes and why, the project manager can better manage these requests and prevent unnecessary changes that could delay the project. **D. Adopt an incremental approach**: This involves breaking down the project into smaller, manageable parts or increments. Each increment delivers a portion of the functionality and can be developed, tested, and delivered independently. This approach allows for better control over the project, as changes can be managed within each increment, reducing the impact on the overall project timeline and budget.

Stakeholder analysis is the process of collecting information about any person that will be impacted by (or can impact) your project. Conducting a stakeholder analysis will enable you to identify all your stakeholders as well as their needs and expectations. The incremental approach is based on the principle that those involved in a project should at the outset focus on the key business objectives that the project is to achieve and be willing to suspend detailed consideration of the minutiae of a selected solution. Kind of like circling the wagons and focusing on the the project at hand.

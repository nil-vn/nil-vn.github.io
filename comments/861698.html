In an enterprise resource planning (ERP) implementation project that spans divisions within and outside the country, a governance framework is crucial. A governance framework provides the structure, processes, and guidelines for decision-making, monitoring, and controlling the project. It helps ensure that the project aligns with the organization's objectives, complies with relevant policies, and meets the needs of diverse stakeholders. The governance framework typically includes elements such as project management plan, policies, procedures, roles and responsibilities, communication plans, and escalation paths. Leveraging a robust governance framework is essential for successful ERP implementations, especially in complex and multinational projects.

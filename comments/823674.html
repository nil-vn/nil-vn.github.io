Selected Answer: A
A potential delay from the supplier is a risk, but before choosing a risk response (accept, mitigate, transfer, etc.), the project manager must first understand the contractual obligations. This includes: Delivery commitments Penalties for late delivery Flexibility or clauses around holiday delays Remedies or escalation paths You cannot insist on delivery (option B) or decide to accept the risk (option D) before confirming what the contract legally allows. This aligns with PMP best practice: When a supplier reports a risk, the project manager's first step is to check the contract before determining the appropriate response. Only after reviewing the agreement can the PM determine whether to: Enforce the delivery date Negotiate adjustments Update the risk register Take mitigating actions

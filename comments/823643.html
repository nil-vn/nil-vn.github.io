C, "Initiate the number of iterations to reduce the number of change requests." In such cases, the project manager can adopt an iterative approach to manage the project effectively. By breaking the project into smaller, manageable iterations, the team can focus on delivering incremental results while continuously learning and adapting based on feedback and changing requirements. Through iterations, the project team can gather valuable insights, test assumptions, and refine the project's direction. This can potentially lead to a reduction in the number of change requests over time as the project evolves based on the gained knowledge and experience.

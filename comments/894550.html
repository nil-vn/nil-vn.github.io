Managing Dependencies: By ensuring that the product backlog is properly prioritized, the project manager can address potential dependencies that may impact the quality of deliverables. Proactive Approach: This approach takes a proactive stance to prevent quality issues rather than reacting to them after they have occurred. By addressing potential dependencies upfront, the project manager can mitigate risks to quality before they impact the project's performance.

In an agile project, the definition of done (DoD) is crucial for ensuring that deliverables meet the required quality standards. If there is a discrepancy in the quality of deliverables, reinforcing and clarifying the DoD during sprint planning is an effective way to address the issue. This action can contribute to improved quality control during the execution of subsequent iterations.

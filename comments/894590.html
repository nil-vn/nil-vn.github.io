Rolling wave planning, as part of an adaptive approach, allows the project team to plan and schedule work based on the information currently available, understanding that further details will be defined as the project progresses. This is especially useful when there's limited information at the outset. Relative estimation, often used in agile methodologies, helps the team determine the size or complexity of tasks or deliverables in relation to one another, rather than trying to pin down exact durations without sufficient detail. This method is beneficial when precise estimations are challenging due to uncertainties. Lastly, using iterations and reviews provides a structured framework for the team to regularly revisit and adapt the plan based on actual progress and emerging information. This iterative process ensures the project remains aligned with objectives and can adjust to any changes or newly recognized requirements.

B. Rolling wave planning and an adaptive approach: Rolling wave planning involves planning for the near term in detail while keeping the long-term planning at a higher level. It's an adaptive approach that allows for adjustments as more information becomes available. D. Iterations and reviews to continuously keep adapting the plan: Using iterative planning and conducting regular reviews allows the project team to adapt the plan as more information becomes available and as the project progresses. It helps in refining the schedule over time. E. Bottom-up estimating based on a work breakdown structure (WBS): Bottom-up estimating involves breaking down the project work into smaller, detailed tasks and estimating the duration and effort for each task. It provides a more accurate estimate when detailed information is available. So, the correct answer for the PMP exam would be B, D, and E. These techniques align with best practices for schedule planning and adaptation in situations with limited information.

Budget estimates are usually high-level estimates done for financial planning purposes. Story points are used in Agile projects to estimate the relative size of a task or a user story. These techniques are not suitable for providing a rough, high-level estimation for a new project with limited details.

Rough order of magnitude (ROM) refers to an initial estimate of the cost of a project or parts of a project. It has an expected accuracy of -25% to +75% according to the PMBOK (other sources suggest -50% to +50%). In other words, the actual costs of a project are typically expected to be between 75% and 175% (or 50% to 150%) of the ROM estimate.

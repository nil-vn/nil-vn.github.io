Selected Answer: A
The best approach for the project manager to address this issue is: A. Start the meeting with a review of the ground rules, meeting objectives, and the agenda. Rationale: Reviewing ground rules at the beginning of the meeting sets clear expectations for behavior, including when and how team members should contribute to the discussion. This reinforces professional and respectful communication and encourages equitable participation. It also allows the project manager to indirectly address disruptive behavior without singling out individuals in the group setting. Why not the other options? • B. Speak individually with the team member who always disrupts the conversations. While this might help in some cases, it doesn’t address the broader need for consistent, group-wide expectations. It also risks creating defensiveness or tension without solving the systemic issue of meeting disruption.

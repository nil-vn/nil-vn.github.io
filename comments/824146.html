One more tricky question. From the requirements-gathering perspective, one-on-one meetings are the best approach for engagement and building consensus. However, conducting personal meetings with 70 individuals seems very disruptive for the organization, time-consuming, and may not be the most efficient approach. Better options would be "group workshops" and "focus groups", but these are not included in the answer options. As a solution, a two-round survey process could be implemented: the first survey to gather preliminary feedback, identify key areas of concern, and generate a list of possible options for the final survey; followed by a second round to collect votes on the options, along with written justifications.

This option offers several advantages: Wide outreach: A well-designed survey can reach all 70 stakeholders efficiently, gathering their perspectives without logistical challenges of a large meeting. Structured data collection: Surveys allow for standardized data collection, making analysis and identifying commonalities easier. Anonymity and comfort: Some stakeholders might feel more comfortable expressing their opinions anonymously through a survey compared to a face-to-face meeting.

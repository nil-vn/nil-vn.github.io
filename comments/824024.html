Technical debt refers to the additional work that will be required in the future due to taking shortcuts or making decisions that prioritize short-term gains over long-term sustainability. Skipping testing can lead to significant technical debt as bugs and errors may be introduced into the software that will require additional work to fix later. Additionally, skipping testing can also lead to reduced quality and user satisfaction, which can ultimately harm the success of the project.

Absolutely, you've highlighted a key aspect of developing a minimum viable product (MVP) – ensuring that it provides value to early adopters. When creating an MVP, it's crucial to focus on delivering features or functionality that address specific pain points or needs of the target market. This helps in validating the product's value proposition and gaining valuable feedback. By understanding the target audience, their pain points, and the value the MVP can deliver, the project manager and the team can make informed decisions about which features to prioritize, thereby ensuring that the MVP serves its purpose effectively. This customer-centric approach is fundamental to the success of an MVP and the overall product development process.

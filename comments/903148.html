According to PMI-PMP, when the product owner meets with multiple stakeholders who have different opinions about the requirements during the user story creation phase, the project manager should take the following action: C. Perform a stakeholder identification analysis. Performing a stakeholder identification analysis will help the project manager to identify the stakeholders who have conflicting requirements and prioritize their needs. This will help the project manager to create a plan to address the conflicting requirements and ensure that all stakeholders are satisfied with the final product. Asking the product owner to create the backlog may not be effective in resolving the conflicting requirements, using a voting system may not be appropriate in this situation, and limiting the participation from stakeholders may not be feasible or desirable.

B. Use a voting system for stakeholders. In situations where multiple stakeholders have different opinions about requirements, using a voting system can help facilitate decision-making and prioritize user stories. This approach involves allowing stakeholders to express their preferences through a voting process, which can be based on factors such as importance, urgency, or alignment with strategic objectives. Using a voting system helps to gather input from various stakeholders, acknowledges their perspectives, and provides a structured way to prioritize user stories. It can contribute to consensus-building and ensure that the most critical or valuable requirements are given priority in the backlog. This approach fosters collaboration and transparency in the decision-making process, enabling the project team to move forward with a clearer understanding of stakeholder priorities.

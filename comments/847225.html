Since the key deliverable now depends on acquiring a new device, there is uncertainty about whether and when it will be obtained. That uncertainty makes this a risk (it may cause delays or scope changes). The project manager should log it in the risk register and escalate it to the project sponsor, who has the authority to approve or facilitate acquisition. Simply reporting the delay (B) happens after the problem materializes — the goal here is to act proactively. Inviting stakeholders to reprioritize (A) might be necessary later, but the immediate step is to formally record and escalate the risk. Assigning acquisition responsibility to the core team (D) might not be feasible if they lack the authority or budget to procure the device.

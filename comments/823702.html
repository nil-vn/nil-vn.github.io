facilitating a problem-solving session with the team is the most effective way to address the technical problem in an agile environment. It empowers the team, promotes collaboration, and allows for a rapid response. While retrospectives are valuable for continuous improvement, they are typically held at the end of a sprint. Holding an early retrospective to address a specific technical problem is less efficient than a focused problem-solving session. A retrospective is for reflecting on the process, not necessarily solving immediate technical roadblocks.

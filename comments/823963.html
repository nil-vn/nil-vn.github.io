Selected Answer: C
The question is about PERFORMANCE. Explanation: Story points are team-specific estimates of effort and complexity, not a universal metric. Comparing story points between teams (e.g., 100 vs. 125) is invalid because each team defines points differently. Performance in Agile is measured by a team’s ability to deliver working increments consistently. The team that completes more stories (assuming similar scope and value) from the sprint backlog demonstrates better delivery efficiency, regardless of story point totals. Why Other Options Are Incorrect: A/B: Story points cannot be compared across teams. Fewer or more points do not inherently indicate better performance. D: While fewer defects reflect QUALITY, the question focuses on PERFORMANCE, which Agile ties to delivering value iteratively.

Correct answer is C: In Agile environments, the performance of a team is not determined solely by the number of story points they calculate but by their ability to deliver completed, working product increments as committed during a sprint. D is incorrect: While quality is important, Agile primarily measures success based on delivering working increments. Quality can complement this, but it is not the sole performance indicator.
